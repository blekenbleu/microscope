Objective comparisons

Initial contemplation of comparing infinity and finite objectives
occured during Nikon finite vs infinity head circular dovetails discussion.
Back in the day, for AO Series 10 usage on objects without coverslips,
Olympus MSPlan objectives were the recommended hot setup,
but I instead first acquired a cheap set of MEIJI S.PLAN M objectives.
While prefering episcopic microscopy, transmissive calibration slides ease comparisons,
with coverslips according to objective engravings.  20x is my favored objective power.

First impressions:
  • 100W Optiphot 2 eyepiece images are uncomfortably bright for < 20x;  view with ND400
  • Image circles are constrained by RMS thread for objectives < 10x power.
  • Image circles are constrained by tube lens aperture for objectives > 10x power.
  • 10x objective images are not circular when objectives are not parcentric with tube lens
  • Sony α7-RII sensor is 35.9mm and 7952 pixels wide
    • From ImageJ - Analyze - Plot Profile, Olympus MSPlan 5x edges are > 10 pixels wide:

      ... while Nikon CF Plan Epi 20x edges are 6 pixels wide:

      ... the limit for minimal aliasing artifacts.
  • Imaging Edge Remote histogram helps condenser diaphragm adjustment
  • Microscope image dynamic ranges are well within JPEG limits; no need for camera RAW.
  • JPEG quality 85 reduces image sizes about 90% from Sony Extra fine with imperceptible impacts.
  • Fine focus by camera lens is nicer than by microscope stage control.
  • EF 70-200 aperture setting strongly influences captured objective image circle.
  • Aligning and centering camera lens as tube lens wants fiddling.
  • Blackening AO/Reichert trinoc photo tube interior addressed flare artifacts.
  • I had forgotton how comfortable are AO 180 eyepieces;
    my eyes feel a decade younger viewing thru them instead of most Nikon CFWs,
    which are nominally interchangeable in my experience.

Other tube lens comparisons

Most famously, by Robert OToole, as discussed on photomacrography.net.
Interestingly, Robert discovered some better results by defocusing an object,
then focussing tube lens beyond infinity,
which also reduced overall magnification to the sensor.
Robert called this "pushed down", reducing tube length between sensor and tube lens.
This worked for him with Raynox DCR-150, with 144mm from sensor to tube lens,
and Sigma Life-Size Attachment Lens (LSA) at 145mm sensor-to-tube lens.
Sigma Life-size about 180mm from 52mm thread to Sony α7R II flange
52-42mm step-down from RAFCAMERA DT48.5-M42
48-52mm step-up from 48mm extension tubes, then
48-42mm step-down + M42 male coupler to M42-Emount focus helicoid

Objective was first focussed using Nikon Eclipse head and eyepieces;
condenser wanted significant refocusing when replacing Eclipse head.
Perhaps "push down" provokes objective aberrations that complement tube lens aberrations,
and might work for other combinations involving tube lens focused at less than infinity...?

In 2021 testing, Robert declared Raynox DCR-150 the best tube lens for Sony α7R IV 35mm sensor,
(208mm sensor to lens, minimal objective spacing), but no camera telephotos were tested.
